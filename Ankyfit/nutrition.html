Nutrition for Ankylosing Spondylitis

Proper nutrition is crucial for managing Ankylosing Spondylitis. A well-balanced diet can help reduce inflammation, maintain healthy weight, and support overall well-being.

Anti-inflammatory Foods

Include foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and phytonutrients to help reduce inflammation.

  • Fatty fish: Salmon, mackerel, and sardines.
  • Leafy greens: Spinach, kale, and Swiss chard.
  • Nuts and seeds: Flaxseeds, chia seeds, and walnuts.

Whole Foods

Focus on whole grains, legumes, fresh fruits, and vegetables to support a healthy diet.

  • Whole grains: Quinoa, brown rice, and whole wheat.
  • Legumes: Lentils, chickpeas, and beans.
  • Colorful vegetables: Carrots, beets, and bell peppers.

Limit Processed Foods and Sugars

Reducing intake of processed foods, sugars, and unhealthy fats can help manage symptoms and prevent complications.

  • Avoid: Sugary snacks, fried foods, and ready-made meals.
  • Limit: Red meat and high-fat dairy products.
