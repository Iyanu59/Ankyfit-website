Ankylosing Spondylitis Specific Workouts

These exercises are designed to help maintain flexibility, improve posture, and lessen the pain associated with Ankylosing Spondylitis. They should be performed under the guidance of your physiotherapist.

Stretching Routines

Regular stretching can help reduce spinal stiffness and maintain range of motion.

  • Wall Press: Stand facing away and lean back against the wall to stretch your back muscles.
  • Pelvic Tilts: Lie on your back with your knees bent and gently rock your pelvis towards your head.
  • Dead Hang: go to a pull up bar and just hang on, for as long as possible. this works and strengthens the upper back
  • Cobra Stretch: Lie down: On your stomach, legs straight, hands under shoulders. Lift: Inhale, press hands down, and lift your chest, keeping hips grounded. Hold: Stay for 15-30 seconds, breathing normally. Lower: Exhale and return to starting position. Repeat: Perform a few times as comfortable.

Strengthening Exercises

Focus on core and back muscles to support and stabilize your spine.

  • Bridge: Lie on your back with knees bent, lift your hips to form a straight line from knees to shoulders.
  • Seated Row: Use a resistance band or rowing machine to strengthen your back and shoulders.
  • pushups:Position in a plank with hands slightly wider than shoulders. Keep your body straight from head to heels, core tight.Bend elbows to lower chest towards the floor. Push back up to extend arms fully. Perform desired repetitions, maintaining form.

Low-Impact Aerobics

Activities like walking, cycling, or swimming can improve cardiovascular health without putting too much strain on the joints.

  • Stationary Cycling: A great way to get your heart rate up without impacting your joints.
  • Aquatic Exercises: Perform exercises in a pool to reduce strain on your joints while providing resistance.
